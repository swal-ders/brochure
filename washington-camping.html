Washington Mount Baker

Mt. Baker offers outdoor enthusiasts a spectacular experience all year round. The sheer size and magnitude allows for many walking trails and intensive hikes for outdoor enthusiasts of all levels. Hiking around and on Mt. Baker is a must do for all hiking newbies and fanatics. The trails have mind-blowing views and spring is a great season to complete a hike here due to the temperature and the beauty that will surround you; whether you’re looking for a hike to do with your family, friends or partner.

Washington Mount Rainer

Mt. Rainier National Park is the fifth national park created in the United States and definitely one of the most stunning places in the country. This expansive park is full of adventures ideal for the most casual group looking for a family camping escape to those who want to summit the Cascade Range’s tallest peak, Mt. Rainier (14,411 feet). Mount Rainier does not disappoint and is just as legendary as featured adventures and photos included here make it out to be. One of the most popular places to summit a 14er using traditional mountaineering equipment and skills, Mount Rainier is a mountaineer’s playground.

Washington Olympic Park

Olympic National Park, the 5th most visited park in the United States, is amazingly still left off the list of many visitors to Washington State. Just a few hours from Seattle, Olympic National Park gives visitors a chance to explore glacier capped mountains, rain forests, and beaches, all within the park boundaries. Called three parks in one by nearly all who visit, a trip to Olympic National Park is a nature experience you'll never forget. While many know of Olympic National Park, few who haven’t visited the region understand what makes it such a sought after destination for hikers, adventurers, families and road trippers from around the world.

Washington Spokane

A mixture of urban chic and outdoor adventures, Spokane has something for everyone. Taste food by James-Beard-nominated chefs at Sante or The Wandering Table. Listen to live music at The Bartlett or the symphony at the iconic Martin Woldson Theatre at The Fox. Glide over the breathtaking Spokane Falls, situated right downtown, in the SkyRide, rated as one of the world’s best gondolas. Kids can feed the Garbage-Eating Goat or slide down the giant Radio Flyer Red Wagon in Riverfront Park.
